Item 8.3.7.5.b.

We use the Ratio Test with \(a_n = \frac{10^n}{n!}\text{.}\) First we consider the ratios of successive terms:

\begin{align*} \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} =\mathstrut \amp \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{ \frac{10^{n+1}}{(n+1)!}}{ \frac{10^{n}}{(n)!} }\\ =\mathstrut \amp \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{10}{n+1}\\ =\mathstrut \amp 0\text{.} \end{align*}

Since the limit of the ratio of successive terms is less than 1, we conclude that the series \(\sum \frac{10^k}{k!}\) converges.

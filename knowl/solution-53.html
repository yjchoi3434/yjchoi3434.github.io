Solution 1.5.4.3.1.
\(W=f(c)\) where \(W\) is weight in pounds and \(c\) is the number of Calories consumed per day: (a)\(f(1700) = 155\) means that consuming 1700 cal each day results in a weight of 155 lb (the units of 1700 are cal, and 155 lb). (b)\(f'(2000)=0\) means that consuming 2000 cal each day causes neither weight gain nor loss (the units of the 2000 are cal, and 0 lb/cal). (c)\(f^{-1}(160)=2500\) means that a weight of 160 lb is caused by a consumption of 2500 cal each day (the units of 160 are lb, and 2500 cal).

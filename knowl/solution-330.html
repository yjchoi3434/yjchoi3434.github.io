Solution 5.4.7.5.1.

  1. By the Second Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, \(F'(x) = f(x) = xe^{-2x}\text{.}\)

  2. To use the First Fundamental Theorem of Calculus to evaluate the integral in \(F(x) = \int_0^x te^{-2t} \, dt\text{,}\) we use integration by parts. Letting

    \begin{align*} u &= t, \ du = dt\\ dv &= e^{-2t} \, dt, \ v = -\frac{1}{2}e^{-2t}\text{,} \end{align*}

    it follows that

    \begin{equation*} \int te^{-2t} \, dt = -\frac{1}{2}te^{-2t} - \int \left( -\frac{1}{2}e^{-2t} \right) \, dt = -\frac{1}{2}te^{-2t} - \frac{1}{4}e^{-2t}\text{.} \end{equation*}

    Thus,

    \begin{align*} F(x) &= \int_0^x te^{-2t} \, dt\\ &= \left. -\frac{1}{2}te^{-2t} - \frac{1}{4}e^{-2t} \right|_{0}^{x}\\ &= -\frac{1}{2}xe^{-2x} - \frac{1}{4}e^{-2x} - \left( 0 - \frac{1}{4}e^{0} \right)\\ &= -\frac{1}{2}xe^{-2x} - \frac{1}{4}e^{-2x} + \frac{1}{4} \end{align*}

  3. For \(x \gt 0\text{,}\) we observe that \(F'(x) = f(x) = xe^{-2x} \gt 0\) since \(e\) to any power is positive. Thus, because \(F'(x) \gt 0\text{,}\) it follows that \(F\) is increasing for all \(x \gt 0\text{.}\)

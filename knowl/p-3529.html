Because the graph of \(g'(x)\) is negative for all \(-2 \lt x \lt 2\text{,}\) we know that the graph of \(g(x)\) must always be decreasing there. The derivative appears to be zero at \(x = \pm 2\text{,}\) so the graph of \(g(x)\) must start out flat at \(x = -2\text{,}\) and then decreases more and more rapidly as it approaches \(x = -1\text{.}\) After \(x = -1\) the it decreases less rapidly, finally flattening out entirely as we get to \(x = 2\text{.}\)

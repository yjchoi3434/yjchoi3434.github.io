(a) As \(x\) approaches \(-1\) from either side, the values of \(f(x)\) get closer and closer to \(5\text{,}\) so the limit appears to be about \(5\text{.}\) (b) As \(x\) approaches 0 from either side, the values of \(f(x)\) get closer and closer to \(6\text{.}\) (Recall that to find a limit, we are interested in what happens to the function near \(x\) but not at \(x\text{.}\)) The limit appears to be about \(6\text{.}\) (c) As \(x\) approaches 1 from either side, the values of \(f(x)\) get closer and closer to \(5\) on one side of \(x = 1\) and get closer and closer to \(-11\) on the other side of \(x = 1\text{.}\) Thus the limit does not exist.

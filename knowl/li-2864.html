Item 4.3.5.8.b.

Using computational technology, we use the midpoint rule to estimate the total change of position, \(\int_0^4 v(t) \, dt\text{.}\) We find that \(M_{25} = 2.6496\text{,}\) \(M_{50} = 2.6624\text{,}\) \(M_{75} = 2.66477\text{,}\) and \(M_{100} = 2.6656\text{.}\) Since it appears that neither of the first two decimal places are changing as we increase the number of subintervals, it looks like \(P = \int_0^4 v(t) \, dt \approx 2.665\) is accurate to two decimal places.

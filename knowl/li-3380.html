Item d.

The curves \(y = x^3-x\) and \(y = x^2\) intersect at three points, forming two regions. Setting \(x^3-x=x^2\) and solving, we find that

\begin{equation*} x^3-x^2-x=x(x^2-x-1)=0\text{,} \end{equation*}

which implies that \(x=0\) or \(x = \frac{1}{2}(1 \pm \sqrt{5})\text{.}\) Plotting the curves, we see that for the left-hand region, the relative interval is \([\frac{1-\sqrt{5}}{2},0]\) and \(g(x)=x^3-x \gt x^2 = f(x)\text{.}\)

Thus, the left-hand region has area

\begin{equation*} A_1 = \int_{\frac{1 - \sqrt{5}}{2}}^0 \left( \left(x^3 - x \right) - x^2\right) dx = \dfrac{13 - 5\sqrt{5}}{24} \approx 0.075819\text{.} \end{equation*}

For the right-hand region, the interval is \([0, \frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}]\) and \(f(x)=x^2 \gt x^3-x = g(x)\text{,}\) so that its area is

\begin{equation*} A_2 = \int_0^{\frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2}} \left( x^2 - \left(x^3 - x \right) \right) dx = \dfrac{13 + 5\sqrt{5}}{24} \approx 1.007514 \text{.} \end{equation*}

Item 2.2.3.2.b.

The linearization \(L(x)\) to \(f\) at \(a = \pi\) is \((x) = f(\pi) + f'(\pi)(x-\pi)\)L. We calculated \(f'(x)\) in part (a) and so \(f'(\pi) = -3\sin(\pi) - 2\cos(\pi) = 2\text{.}\) We also have \(f(\pi) = 3\cos(\pi) - 2\sin(\pi) + 6 = 3\text{.}\) Hence \(L(x) = 3+2(x-\pi)\text{.}\)

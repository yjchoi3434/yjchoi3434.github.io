Item 2.5.5.8.a.

Since \(h(x) = f(g(x))\text{,}\) by the chain rule we know that \(h'(x) = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)\text{.}\) Using the facts that \(f(x)=x^3\) and \(g(x) = \sin(x)\text{,}\) it follows \(f'(x) = 3x^2\) and \(f'(g(x)) = 3\sin^2(x)\text{,}\) and \(g'(x) = \cos(x)\text{.}\) Therefore,

\begin{equation*} h'(x) = 3\sin^2(x) \cdot \cos(x) \end{equation*}

and \(h'\left( \frac{\pi}{4} \right) = 3 \left( \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \right)^2 \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{3}{2\sqrt{2}}\text{.}\)

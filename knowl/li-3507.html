Item c.

Because we are revolving about \(x = -1\text{,}\) when we slice perpendicular to this we get washers of thickness \(\Delta y\text{,}\) and thus we need to write the two curves as functions of \(y\) in order to determine the radii of the slices as functions of \(y\text{,}\) we note that the line can be expressed as \(x = \frac{1}{2}y\) and the cubic curve can be rewritten in the form \(x = y^{1/3}\text{.}\) The outer radius of a typical slice is thus \(R(y) = y^{1/3} + 1\) and the inner radius is \(r(y) = \frac{1}{2}y + 1\text{.}\) It follows that the volume of the solid is

\begin{equation*} V = \int_{0}^{2\sqrt{2}} \pi( (y^{1/3} + 1)^2 - (\frac{1}{2}y + 1)^2 ) \, dy = \frac{2}{15}(15 + 8\sqrt{2}) \pi \approx 11.022\text{.} \end{equation*}

We now observe that two familiar sums have arisen. The left Riemann sum \(L_3\) is \(L_3 = f(x_0) \Delta x + f(x_1) \Delta x + f(x_2) \Delta x\text{,}\) and the right Riemann sum is \(R_3 = f(x_1) \Delta x + f(x_2) \Delta x + f(x_3) \Delta x\text{.}\) Substituting \(L_3\) and \(R_3\) for the corresponding expressions in EquationÂ (5.6.4), it follows that \(T_3 = \frac{1}{2} \left[ L_3 + R_3 \right]\text{.}\) We have thus seen a very important result: using trapezoids to estimate the (signed) area bounded by a curve is the same as averaging the estimates generated by using left and right endpoints.

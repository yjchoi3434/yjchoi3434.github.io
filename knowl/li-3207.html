Item 5.2.5.5.d.

To find where the total sand function \(Y(x)\) has its minimum value on \([0,6]\text{,}\) we look at the behavior of \(Y'(x) = S(x) - R(x)\text{.}\) Plotting \(Y'(x)\) on the interval, we see that \(Y'(x) = 0\) at \(x \approx 5.118\text{,}\) and for \(0 \le x \lt 5.118\text{,}\) \(Y'(x) \lt 0\text{,}\) and for \(5.118 \lt x \le 6\text{,}\) \(Y'(x) \gt 0\text{.}\) Thus, \(Y\) changes from decreasing to increasing at \(x \approx 5.118\) and has an absolute minimum there on the interval \([0,6]\text{.}\) Furthermore, \(Y(5.118) \approx 2492.368\text{.}\)

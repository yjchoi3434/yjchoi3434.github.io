Differentiating implicitly with respect to \(x\) gives

\begin{equation*} 5 y^3 + 15 x y^2 \, \frac{dy}{dx} + y + x \, \frac{dy}{dx} = 0. \end{equation*}

If \(x = 5\) and \(y = 1\text{,}\) then this equation becomes

\begin{equation*} 6 + 80 \, \frac{dy}{dx} = 0, \qquad \text{and so} \qquad \frac{dy}{dx} = -{\textstyle\frac{3}{40}}. \end{equation*}

If \((x, y)\) is any point on the tangent line besides \(\left(5,1\right)\) then

\begin{equation*} -{\textstyle\frac{3}{40}} = \frac{y-1}{x-5}, \qquad \text{or} \qquad 3\!\left(x-5\right) = -40\!\left(y-1\right). \end{equation*}

Therefore, \(3x+40y = 55\) is an equation of the line tangent to the curve at the point \(\left(5,1\right)\text{.}\)

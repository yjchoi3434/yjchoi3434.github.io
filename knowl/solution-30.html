Solution 1.3.3.3.1.
A. Since \(f\) is increasing, \(f(12)\) < \(f(15)\text{.}\) B. From the figure, it appears that \(f(12) - f(9)\) > \(f(9) - f(6)\text{.}\) C. The quantity \(\frac{f(9)-f(6)}{9-6}\) represents the slope of the secant line connecting the points on the graph at \(x=6\) and \(x=9\text{.}\) This is less than the slope of the secant line connecting the points at \(x=6\) and \(x=12\text{,}\) which is \(\frac{f(12)-f(6)}{12-6}\)D. The function is less steep at \(x=6\) than at \(x=15\text{,}\) so \(f'(6)\) < \(f'(15)\)

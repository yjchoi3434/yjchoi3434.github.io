Solution 1.6.5.6.1.

  1. Since \(f'(2)\) is positive, \(f\) is increasing near \(x=2\text{,}\) and since \(f''(2)\) is negative, \(f\) is concave down near \(x=2\text{.}\)

  2. We are given that \(f(2) = -3\text{.}\) Since \(f\) is increasing at \(x=2\text{,}\) we expect \(f(2.1)\) to be greater than \(f(2) = -3\text{.}\)

  3. Since \(f''(2)\) is negative, we also know that \(f'\) is decreasing near \(2\text{.}\) Thus, we expect that \(f'(2.1)\) will be less than \(f'(2) = 1.5\text{.}\)

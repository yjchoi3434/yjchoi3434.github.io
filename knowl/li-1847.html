Item 2.6.6.11.b.

To find a formula for \(g = f^{-1}\text{,}\) we take the equation \(y = \frac{1}{4}x^3 + 4\) and solve for \(x\) in terms of \(y\text{.}\) Doing so, we find that \(y - 4 = \frac{1}{4}x^3\text{,}\) so \(x^3 = 4(y-4)\text{,}\) and thus

\begin{equation*} x = \sqrt[3]{4(y-4)} = f^{-1}(y)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Writing the inverse as a function of \(x\text{,}\) we have found that \(f^{-1}(x) = g(x) = \sqrt[3]{4x-16}\text{.}\)

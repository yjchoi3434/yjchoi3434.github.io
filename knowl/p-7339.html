  1. Since \(a_n = 1 + \frac{1}{2^n} \to 1 \ne 0\) and \(b_n = -1 \to -1 \ne 0\) as \(n \to \infty\text{,}\) by the Divergence Test both \(\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} a_k\) and \(\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} b_k\) diverge.

  2. However, the series

    \begin{equation*} \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (a_k+b_k) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (1 + \frac{1}{2^k} - 1) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{1}{2^k} \end{equation*}

    converges because the series is geometric with \(r = \frac{1}{2}\text{.}\)

  3. The issue is that it doesn't make any sense to talk about

    \begin{equation*} \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} a_k + \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} b_k\text{.} \end{equation*}

    when \(\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} a_k\) and \(\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} b_k\) diverge. Since those series diverge, neither sum is a finite number, so it doesn't make any sense to add them.

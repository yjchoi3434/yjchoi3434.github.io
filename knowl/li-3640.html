Item e.

Since \(p(x) = 4e^{0.020732x}\) lies below \(\rho(x) = 4 + 0.1x\) up until where they intersect at about \(17.53\text{,}\) after which \(p(x) \gt \rho(x)\text{,}\) we see that \(p(x)\) distributes mass more to the right. It also appears as if the two curves may bound the same total area, and thus the generate the same total mass. If that is the case, we expect \(\overline{x}\) for \(p(x)\) to lie slightly to the right of the center of mass for \(\rho(x)\text{.}\)

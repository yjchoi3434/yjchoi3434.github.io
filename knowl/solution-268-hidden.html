A. The integral is the area above the \(x\)-axis minus the area below the \(x\)-axis. Thus, we can see that \(\int_{-3}^3 f(x)\,dx\) is about \(-7.25 + 3.75 = -3.5\text{.}\) To determine which average value of \(f(x)\) is largest, we consider the areas \(\int_{-3}^{3} f(x) dx\) and \(\int_{-3}^{0} f(x) dx\text{.}\) If the first (divided by six) is greater than the second (divided by 3), the average value over the first is larger than that over the second.

Given \(p(x) = x^3 - a^2x\) on \([0,a]\text{,}\) we first find the critical number(s) of \(p\) that lie in \([0,a]\text{.}\) Since \(p'(x) = 3x^2 - a^2 = 3\left(x^2 - \frac{a^2}{3} \right)\text{,}\) we know \(p\) has critical numbers at \(x = \pm \frac{a}{\sqrt{3}}\text{.}\) The negative critical number lies outside the interval, while the positive one lies within the interval since \(0 \lt \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \lt 1\text{.}\) Evaluating \(p\) at the endpoints and the critical number within the interval, we find that \(p(0) = 0\text{,}\) \(p(a) = a^3 - a^2 \cdot a = 0\text{,}\) and \(p\left( \frac{a}{\sqrt{3}} \right) = \left( \frac{a}{\sqrt{3}} \right)^3 - a^2 \cdot \left( \frac{a}{\sqrt{3}} \right) = \frac{a^3}{3\sqrt{3}} - \frac{a^3}{\sqrt{3}} = -\frac{2a^3}{3\sqrt{3}}\text{.}\) Hence, the absolute maximum of \(p\) on \([0,a]\) is \(p(0) = p(a) = 0\) and the absolute minimum is \(p\left( \frac{a}{\sqrt{3}} \right) = -\frac{2a^3}{3\sqrt{3}}\text{.}\)

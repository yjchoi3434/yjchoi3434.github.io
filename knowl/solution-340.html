Solution 5.5.5.5.1.

SOLUTION

Multiplying by the least common denominator gives

\begin{equation*} 2x^{2}-5x+23 = A(x^2+9)+(Bx+C)(x - 1) \end{equation*}

Rearranging terms on the right hand side, yields

\begin{equation*} 2x^{2}-5x+23 = (A+B)x^2+(-B+C)x + 9 A - C \end{equation*}

Now we equate the coefficients:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rl} A+B \amp = 2 \\ -B +C \amp = -5 \\ 9 A - C \amp = 23 \end{array} \end{equation*}

Solving the system gives \(A=2, \; B = 0\) and \(C= -5\) so the partial fraction decomposition is

\begin{equation*} \frac{2x^{2}-5x+23}{(x - 1)(x^2+9)} = \frac{2}{x - 1} + \frac{-5}{x^2+9} \end{equation*}

The definite integral is then

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \int \frac{2x^{2}-5x+23}{(x - 1)(x^2+9)} dx = 2\ln\!\left(\left|x-1\right|\right) + \frac{-5\tan^{-1}\!\left(\frac{x}{3}\right)}{3} +C \end{equation*}

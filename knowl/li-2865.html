Item 4.3.5.8.c.

We noted in (a) where \(v(t)\) is positive and negative. In particular, on \(1 \lt t \lt 3\text{,}\) \(v(t) \lt 0\text{,}\) and thus \(\int_1^3 v(t) \, dt\) will give us a negative change in position; the distance traveled on this interval is therefore \(-\int_1^3 v(t) \, dt\text{,}\) and hence the total distance traveled, \(D\text{,}\) on \([0,4]\) is

\begin{equation*} D = \int_0^1 v(t) \, dt - \int_1^3 v(t) \, dt + \int_3^4 v(t) \, dt\text{.} \end{equation*}

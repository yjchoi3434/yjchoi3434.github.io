Item 4.3.5.9.b.

The value of \(\int_1^4 f(x) \,dx\) is the difference of the areas of the regions \(f\) bounds above the \(x\)-axis minus the areas of the regions \(f\) bounds below the \(x\)-axis on the interval \([1,4]\text{.}\) The area of the triangle \(f\) bounds above the \(x\)-axis on the interval \([1,1.5]\) is \((0.5)(2)(0.5) = 0.5\text{,}\) the area of the region \(f\) bounds below the \(x\)-axis on \([1.5,2]\) is also \(0.5\text{,}\) the area of the region \(f\) bounds below the \(x\)-axis on \([2,3]\) is the area (\(2\)) of a rectangle with height \(2\) and base \(1\text{,}\) and the area of the region \(f\) bounds below the \(x\)-axis on \([3,4]\) is the area (\(1\)) of a triangle with height \(2\) and base \(1\text{.}\) So

\begin{equation*} \int_1^4 f(x) \,dx = 0.5-0.5-2-1 = -3\text{.} \end{equation*}

Notice that the graph of \(g\) is linear on the interval \([1,2]\text{,}\) passing through the points \((1,-1)\) and \((2,2)\text{.}\) This line has slope \(3\) and equation \(y = -1+3(x-1)\text{.}\) The \(x\)-intercept of this line is \(\frac{4}{3}\text{.}\) So the triangle the graph of \(g\) bounds on the interval \(\left[1, \frac{4}{3}\right]\) has area \(\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)(1) = \frac{1}{6}\text{,}\) and the area of the triangle between the graph of \(g\) and the \(x\)-axis on the interval \(\left[\frac{4}{3}, 2\right]\) is \(\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)(2) = \frac{2}{3}\text{.}\) Finally, the graph of \(g\) bounds a quarter circle of radius \(2\) on the interval \([2,4]\text{,}\) so

\begin{equation*} \int_1^4 g(x) \,dx = -\frac{1}{6} + \frac{2}{3} + \pi = \frac{1}{2} + \pi\text{.} \end{equation*}

Therefore,

\begin{equation*} \int_1^4 [2f(x) - 3g(x)] \, dx = 2\int_1^4 f(x) \, dx - 3\int_1^4 g(x) \, dx = (2)(-3) - (3)\left(\frac{1}{2} + \pi\right) = -\frac{15}{2} - 3\pi\text{.} \end{equation*}

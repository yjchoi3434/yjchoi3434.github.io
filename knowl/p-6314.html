In FigureÂ 6.1.3, we see that the graphs intersect at \((0,2)\) and \((3,5)\text{.}\) We can find these intersection points algebraically by solving the system of equations given by \(y = x+2\) and \(y = (x-1)^2 + 1\text{:}\) substituting \(x+2\) for \(y\) in the second equation yields \(x+2 = (x-1)^2 + 1\text{,}\) so \(x+2 = x^2 - 2x + 1 + 1\text{,}\) and thus

\begin{equation*} x^2 - 3x = x(x-3) = 0\text{,} \end{equation*}

from which it follows that \(x = 0\) or \(x = 3\text{.}\) Using \(y = x+2\text{,}\) we find the corresponding \(y\)-values of the intersection points.

Evaluating \(r\) at the endpoints and the critical number, we find

  • \(\displaystyle r\left( \frac{1}{2b} \right) = a \cdot \frac{1}{2b} \cdot e^{-1/2} \approx 0.607 \frac{a}{2b} = 0.3035 \frac{a}{b}\)

  • \(\displaystyle r\left( \frac{2}{b} \right) = a \cdot \frac{2}{b} \cdot e^{-2} \approx 0.135 \frac{2a}{b} = 0.270 \frac{a}{b}\)

  • \(\displaystyle r\left( \frac{1}{b} \right) = a \cdot \frac{1}{b} \cdot e^{-1} \approx 0.368 \frac{a}{b} \)

The absolute max of \(r\) is therefore \(r\left( \frac{1}{b} \right) \approx 0.368 \frac{a}{b}\) and the absolute min is \(r\left( \frac{2}{b} \right) \approx 0.270 \frac{a}{b}\text{.}\)

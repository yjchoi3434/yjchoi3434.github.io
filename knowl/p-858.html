For \(a = -2\text{,}\) \(a = -1\text{,}\) and \(a = 1\text{,}\) \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) \ne f(a)\text{.}\) At \(a = -2\text{,}\) the limit fails to exist, but \(f(-2) = 1\text{.}\) At \(a = -1\text{,}\) the limit is \(\frac{5}{3}\text{,}\) but \(f(-1)\) is not defined. At \(a = 1\text{,}\) the limit is 3, but \(f(1) = 2\text{.}\)

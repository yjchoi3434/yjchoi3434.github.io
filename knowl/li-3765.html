Item e.

After 4 seconds traveling at 7 cm/sec, the particle has moved 28 cm. So, we have to find the value of \(b\) for which the arc length \(L\) along \([0,b]\) is 28. Thus, we know that

\begin{equation*} 28 = L = \int_0^b \sqrt{1 + (0.2x)^2} \, dx\text{.} \end{equation*}

Rewriting the integral, we see

\begin{equation*} 28 = \int_0^b \sqrt{1+0.04x^2} \, dx\text{.} \end{equation*}

Experimenting with different values of \(b\) in a computational engine like WolframAlpha, we find that for \(b \approx 14.9165\text{,}\) \(\int_0^{14.9165} \sqrt{1+0.04x^2} \, dx \approx 27.9998\text{,}\) and thus the position of the particle when \(t=4\) is approximately \((14.9165,f(14.9165)) = (14.9165, 23.2502)\text{.}\)

On the interval from \(t = \frac{1}{2}\) to \(t = 1\text{,}\) the corresponding area under the velocity curve is the area of the right triangular region whose width is \(\frac{1}{2}\) seconds and whose height is \(v(\frac{1}{2}) = 16\) feet/sec. That area is therefore \(A = \frac{1}{2} bh = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2} \cdot 16 = 4\) feet. This is the total distance the ball traveled vertically on \([\frac{1}{2},1]\text{.}\)

Exercise 2. A cost function.

The cost, \(C\) (in dollars) to produce \(g\) gallons of ice cream can be expressed as \(C = f(g)\text{.}\)

(a) In the expression \(f(150) = 375\text{,}\)

what are the units of 150?

  • dollars

  • gallons

  • dollars*gallons

  • dollars/gallon

  • gallons/dollar

what are the units of 375?

  • dollars

  • gallons

  • dollars*gallons

  • dollars/gallon

  • gallons/dollar

(b) In the expression \(f'(150) = 1.6\text{,}\)

what are the units of 150?

  • dollars

  • gallons

  • dollars*gallons

  • dollars/gallon

  • gallons/dollar

what are the units of 1.6?

  • dollars

  • gallons

  • dollars*gallons

  • dollars/gallon

  • gallons/dollar

(Be sure that you can carefully put into words the meanings of each of these statement in terms of ice cream and money.)

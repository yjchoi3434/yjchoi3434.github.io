1. Since \(\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (-1)^{k} \frac{1}{2k+1}\) is an alternating series and \(\frac{1}{2k+1} \to 0\text{,}\) the series converges by the Alternating Series Test. However, the corresponding series of positive terms \(\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{1}{2k+1}\) diverges by comparison to the Harmonic series.

  2. \begin{equation*} |S_{100} - \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (-1)^{k} \frac{1}{2k+1}| \lt a_{101} = \frac{1}{2 \cdot 101 +1} \approx 0.0049 \end{equation*}

    is a considerable amount of error when trying to estimate \(\frac{\pi}{4}\) accurately.

  3. In this case we have

    \begin{equation*} |S_n - \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (-1)^{k} \frac{1}{2k+1}| \lt a_{n+1} = \frac{1}{2n+1}\text{.} \end{equation*}

    To have \(a_{n+1} \lt 10^{-10}\) would require

    \begin{align*} \frac{1}{2n+1} \amp \lt 10^{-10}\\ 2n+1 \amp \gt 10^{10}\\ n \amp \gt \frac{10^{10}-1}{2}\\ n \amp \gt 4{,}999{,}999{,}999.5\text{.} \end{align*}

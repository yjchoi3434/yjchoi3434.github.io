Item 7.6.4.6.c.

The bacteria is increasing most rapidly when \(\frac{db}{dt}\) is greatest. Since

\begin{equation*} \frac{db}{dt} = \frac{1}{3000} b(15000 - b) \end{equation*}

is a quadratic function of \(b\text{,}\) \(\frac{db}{dt}\) is maximized at the vertex of this concave down parabola, which occurs halfway between its zeros: \(b = 0\) and \(b = 15000\text{.}\) Thus, when \(b = 7500\text{,}\) the population is growing fastest. To find the exact \(t\)-value when this occurs, we'd need to know an initial condition like in the next part of the problem.

Solution 6.1.5.2.1.
Over the interval \([0,\pi]\text{,}\) the graphs \(y = 2\sin x\) and \(y = 8 \cos x\) intersect when \(2\sin x = 8 \cos x\text{,}\) i.e., \(\tan x = \frac{8}{2}\text{,}\) i.e., \(x=\tan^{-1}(\frac{8}{2})=1.32581766366803\text{.}\) Let \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{8}{2})\text{.}\) Over the interval \(\left[0, t\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 2\sin x\) lies below that of \(y = 8 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int_0^{ t}(8 \cos x-2 \sin x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =\left.(8\sin x+2\cos x)\right|_0^{ t} \cr \amp =\left(8\sin t+2\cos t\right)-(8\cdot 0+2\cdot 1)\cr \amp =\left(8\sin t+2\cos t\right)-2 \end{array} \end{equation*}
Since \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{8}{2})\text{,}\) we have \(\tan t = \frac{8}{2}\text{.}\) Considering a rightangle triangle with an angle which opposite side has length 8 and adjacent side has length 2, we see that the hypotenues is \(\sqrt{68}\text{.}\) Thus \(\sin t = \frac{8}{\sqrt{68}}\) and \(\cos t = \frac{2}{\sqrt{68}}\text{.}\) \(8\sin t+2\cos t = \frac{8^2}{\sqrt{68}} + \frac{2^2}{\sqrt{68}} = \frac{8^2+2^2}{\sqrt{68}} = \frac{68}{\sqrt{68}}=\sqrt{68}\text{.}\) Thus, \(\int_0^{ t}(8 \cos x-2 \sin x)dx= \sqrt{68}-2.\) Over the interval \(\left[ t,\pi\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 2\sin x\) lies above that of \(y = 8 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int^\pi_{ t}(2\sin x - 8 \cos x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =-\left.(8\sin x+2\cos x)\right|^\pi_{ t} \cr \amp =2 + \sqrt{68} \end{array} \end{equation*}
Thus the total area is the sum of the above two integrals, which is \(2\sqrt{68}\)

Exercise 2.

Let \(f(x) = 3\cos(x) - 2\sin(x) + 6\text{.}\)

  1. Determine the exact slope of the tangent line to \(y = f(x)\) at the point where \(a = \frac{\pi}{4}\text{.}\)

  2. Determine the tangent line approximation to \(y = f(x)\) at the point where \(a = \pi\text{.}\)

  3. At the point where \(a = \frac{\pi}{2}\text{,}\) is \(f\) increasing, decreasing, or neither?

  4. At the point where \(a = \frac{3\pi}{2}\text{,}\) does the tangent line to \(y = f(x)\) lie above the curve, below the curve, or neither? How can you answer this question without even graphing the function or the tangent line?

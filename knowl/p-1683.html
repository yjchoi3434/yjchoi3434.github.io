The tangent line approximation at \(a = 2\) is \(L(x) = f(2) + f'(2)(x-2) \approx -3 + 0.01832(x-2)\text{.}\) Thus, \(f(1.88) \approx L(1.88) \approx -3 + 0.01832(1.88-2) = -3.00022\text{.}\) Since \(f''(x) \lt 0\) at and around \(x = 2\text{,}\) \(f\) is concave down there, and therefore the tangent line lies above the curve. This makes our estimate larger than the true value of \(f(1.88)\text{.}\)

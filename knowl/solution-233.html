Solution 3.5.3.1.1.

We are given that the diameter and height are always the same, so \(2r = h\text{,}\)or \(r = \frac12 h\text{.}\) Hence, the volume of the pile is

\begin{equation*} \textstyle V = \frac13 \pi r^2 h = \frac13 \pi \bigl( \frac12 h \bigr)^2 h = \frac1{12} \pi h^3. \end{equation*}

Differentiating with respect to time (\(t\)) gives

\begin{equation*} \frac{dV}{dt} = {\textstyle \frac{1}{12} \pi \, 3 h^2} \, \frac{dh}{dt} = {\textstyle \frac14 \pi h^2} \, \frac{dh}{dt}, \end{equation*}

and so

\begin{equation*} \frac{dh}{dt} = \frac{4}{\pi h^2} \frac{dV}{dt}. \end{equation*}

If \(\frac{dV}{dt} = 30\) and \(h = 16\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \frac{dh}{dt} = \frac{4 \cdot 30}{16 ^2 \pi} = \frac{15}{32\pi }. \end{equation*}

Hence, when the pile is \(16\) feet high its height is increasing at a rate of \(\frac{15}{32\pi }\) feet per minute.

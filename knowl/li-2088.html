Item 3.3.4.3.d.

The given information forces the graph of \(p\) to decrease on \((a,c)\) then increase on \((c,b)\text{.}\) Thus, we must have \(p(c) \lt p(b)\) and so \(p\) has its global minimum value at \(x=c\text{.}\) Since \(p(a) \gt p(b)\text{,}\) this makes \(p(a)\) the global maximum value of \(p\) on \([a,b]\text{.}\)

First, \(P'(h) = \frac{dP}{dh} = 30 e^{-0.0000323h} (-0.0000323)\text{.}\) Therefore, \(P'(1000) = 30 e^{-0.0323} (-0.0000323) \approx -0.000938\) inches of mercury per foot. This tells us that the barometric pressure is dropping very slightly for each additional foot of elevation gain.

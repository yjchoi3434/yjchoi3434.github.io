Since the length of the base is twice the width, the volume of the container is

\begin{equation*} V= (2w)(w)h =2 w^2 h = 30 \end{equation*}

so

\begin{equation*} h = \frac{30}{ 2 w^2} \end{equation*}

The sides have total area \(2(2w)h+ 2wh = 6wh\text{,}\) while the base has area \(w(2w) = 2 w^2\text{,}\) so the cost is

\begin{equation*} 5 \cdot (6wh ) + 14\cdot (2 w^2) \end{equation*}

Substuting the expression for \(h\) gives

\begin{equation*} C(w) = 5 \cdot 6 w\cdot \frac{30}{ 2 w^2} + 14\cdot( 2 w^2 ) = \frac{450}{w} + 28 w^2 \quad w >0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} C'(w) = -\frac{450}{w^2} + 56 w = \frac{-450+ 56 w^3}{w^2} \end{equation*}

\(\displaystyle w = \left({\textstyle\frac{225}{28}}\right)^{1/3}\) is the critical number. There is an absolute minimum at this critical number since \(C'(w) \lt 0\) for \(0 \lt w \lt \left({\textstyle\frac{225}{28}}\right)^{1/3}\) and \(C'(w) >0\) for \(w > \left({\textstyle\frac{225}{28}}\right)^{1/3}\text{.}\)
